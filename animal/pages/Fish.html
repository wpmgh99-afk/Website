Fish

Fish are aquatic animals that live in water and breathe with gills. They come in many different shapes and sizes, and can be found in both freshwater and saltwater environments. Some fish are solitary, while others live in schools. Fish play an important role in their ecosystems by serving as both predators and prey.

The rapid decline of wild fish is primarily driven by habitat loss, overfishing, and pollution. As aquatic ecosystems are degraded, fish lose the diverse habitats they need to survive, forcing them into smaller, isolated populations where they are more vulnerable to extinction. Furthermore, they are relentlessly targeted by commercial and recreational fisheries, leading to unsustainable harvesting practices. To protect these vital species, global conservation efforts must focus on restoring and protecting aquatic habitats, implementing sustainable fishing practices, and reducing pollution in waterways to foster healthy fish populations.
