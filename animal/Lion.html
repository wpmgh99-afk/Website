Lions

The lion (Panthera leo), often called the "King of the Jungle," is a magnificent apex predator known for its strength, majestic mane, and powerful roar that can be heard up to five miles away. Unlike most other wild cats, lions are highly social animals that live in family groups called prides, which typically consist of related females, their offspring, and a small coalition of resident males. Living primarily in the grasslands and savannas of sub-Saharan Africa, these big cats rely on cooperative teamwork to hunt large prey like wildebeests, zebras, and buffaloes. While the lionesses do the majority of the hunting and cub-rearing, the heavy-maned males focus on patrolling and defending the pride's territory from rival predators.

The wild lion population is rapidly declining, dropping to around 20,000 individuals due to habitat loss from expanding human agriculture, human-wildlife conflict when lions target livestock, and illegal poaching. To protect them, we must support community-led conservation programs that help local farmers build predator-proof corrals, secure and fund national parks to deter poachers, and establish safe wildlife corridors so isolated prides can migrate and maintain genetic health.
